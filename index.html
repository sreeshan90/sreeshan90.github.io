Use MoneyGram or Western Union

MoneyGram and Western Union are companies that allow you to send and receive money via through a local office.

Pros:

  • Funds are usually available within 10 minutes of deposit

Cons:

  • Large fees when transferring to other countries
  • Non-competitive exchange rates

Use a Check

Personal checks are used to draw money from a bank account. Checks are available from most every bank.

Pros:

  • No fees when used within the United States
  • Funds are available within 5 days or less
  • Widely used and available

Cons:

  • Not accepted by everyone (why?)

Money Order

Money Orders are pre-purchased vouchers for a set amount of money or foreign currency

Pros:

  • Cheapest way to send money (about $10 dollars)
  • Can be purchased at a number of locations (Post Office, UPS, and more)
  • Easy to exchange for cash at numerous locations

Cons:

  • Not accepted in all countries ( which ones?)
  • Requires valid identification to cash
